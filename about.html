Holi

One of the most popular festivals in Asia is Holi. Holi typically falls in March, on the Hindu calendar’s full moon. Known as the “festival of colour”, “festival of spring” and “festival of love”, Holi is an ancient Hindu event that takes place across India celebrating the start of spring.

Loi Krathong

Loi Krathong, takes place on the evening of the full moon of the 12th month in the Thai lunar calendar. During Loi Krathong, people decorate baskets, fill them with candles and float them along rivers. People also light up the sky with lanterns, colourful lights on houses and firework displays.

Dragon Boat Festival

In China, the Dragon Boat Festival is celebrated on the fifth day of the fifth month of the Lunar calendar, usually falling in June. The tradition is said to have originated in ancient China and commemorates the life and death of famous Chinese scholar, Qu Yuan.

Diwali

One of the largest festivals in Asia is Diwali, also known as the “festival of lights”. Observed by Hindus, Sikhs and Jains around the world, it commemorates the triumph of good over evil.

Tet Nguyen Dan

Tet is Vietnamese New Year and means “The Feast of the First Morning of the First Day”. It’s a popular holiday to gather with friends and family and enjoy a feast of special foods like sticky rice cakes.
